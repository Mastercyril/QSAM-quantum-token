Abstract

We present Quantum Superposition Amplitude Modulation (QSAM), a novel framework that leverages quantum mechanical superposition and amplitude amplification to accelerate Bitcoin SHA-256 mining. Our system integrates CHSH Bell-state inequality verification for hardware validation, achieving demonstrated speedups of 481x and 381x over classical baselines on IBM Quantum Heron processors. We further introduce the Universal Quantum Codex — a single-parameter force unification framework yielding UC = 3.76 × 10⁻⁵ — for noise-adaptive circuit optimization, a biologically-inspired decaying memory architecture, and a self-evolving AI framework (Q Genesis). Experimental results across 63 quantum jobs on IBM backends demonstrate sustained mining performance with unified confidence UC = 3.76 × 10⁻⁵.
